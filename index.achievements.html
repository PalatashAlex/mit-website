Computer modeling of solar eclipses in Transcarpathia in the past and future

Description: The work examines the fundamentals of the theory of solar eclipses, the physical nature of the Moon, its motion, and its role in the occurrence of different types of eclipses. It also provides a detailed description of astronomical phenomenon modeling, in particular the algorithm for finding eclipses using the electronic planetarium REDSHIFT-7, with accompanying tables and maps of eclipse circumstances. Using another electronic planetarium, STELLARIUM-0.12.4, visualizations of the main phases of several total, annular, and partial eclipses of the past and future were created. Detailed lists and maps are provided for some total, annular, and partial eclipses that occurred in Transcarpathia over the past 350 years and will occur in the next 150 years. The relevance of this work lies in demonstrating the importance not only of astronomical knowledge but also of the ability to apply it for reconstructing historical CVCALLDÔºö

The positive aspects: systematic and coherent presentation of material, independently conducted research, and the ability to work in library archives, obtaining valuable information.

Motivation: Solar eclipses are among the most fascinating astronomical events. I wanted not only to study their physics but also to show how astronomy can explain historical events and inspire students to explore space.

Process:

  • Modeled eclipses with REDSHIFT-7 and generated tables and maps of their circumstances
  • Used STELLARIUM-012.4 to visualize eclipse phases
  • Analyzed historical texts (Huklyvskyi Chronicle, Song of Terrible Years, 19th-century "Mesiatsoslov") to find cultural mentions of eclipses
  • Compared results of simulations with historical evidence

Results:

  • Produced complete chronology of eclipses for 500 years (350 past, 150 future)
  • Generated visualizations and maps for full, annular, and partial eclipses
  • Verified historical sources by aligning astronomical predictions with documented events

Traction motor anti-wheelslip protection device

Description: In this work, I summarized the available solutions to the problem of wheelslip on Ukraine‚Äôs railways and proposed my own ideas in this area. By analyzing existing solutions, I identified shortcomings in the traction motor anti-wheelslip protection system. The wheelslip protection system on locomotives‚Äîimplemented as a wheelslip relay (WSR)‚Äîin certain situations cannot prevent motor runaway. Therefore, I set myself the goal of developing a scheme to protect the electric motor from runaway. This time, I worked not only on the theoretical part but also aimed to demonstrate an experimental prototype. To achieve this, I found a suitable place for experiments and gathered the necessary materials. In the end, I was able to design and build a tangible model of the development. The positive aspects of this research work include the systematic and coherent presentation of material, independently conducted research, and the ability to work in institutions such as depots, consulting with specialists in this field.

Motivation: After the success of my previous work, I decided to continue the project. This time, I focused on electric locomotives and created an experimental model of the device.

Process:

Results:
